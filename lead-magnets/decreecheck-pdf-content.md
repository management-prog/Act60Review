## PAGE 3 — Residency & Presence (Items 1-7)

**Section: Residency & Presence Requirements**

[ ] **1. 183-Day Presence Test [!]**
I have a contemporaneous log documenting my physical presence in Puerto Rico for at least 183 days during the tax year. This log is supported by corroborating evidence (travel records, credit card statements, flight itineraries).

[ ] **2. Tax Home Test [!]**
My principal place of business and primary office location are in Puerto Rico. I can document that I conduct the majority of my business activities from PR.

[ ] **3. Closer Connection Test [!]**
I maintain stronger ties to Puerto Rico than to any other jurisdiction. My voter registration, driver's license, primary bank accounts, and principal residence are all in PR.

[ ] **4. Form 8898 Filed**
I filed Form 8898 (Statement for Individuals Who Begin or End Bona Fide Residence in a US Possession) for the year I established PR residency, or I have confirmed it was filed in a prior year.

[ ] **5. No Extended Absences**
I did not spend more than 90 consecutive days outside of Puerto Rico during the tax year, or if I did, I can document the reason and demonstrate it did not affect my bona fide residency status.

[ ] **6. Travel Records Retained**
I have retained flight records, passport stamps, or other travel documentation that corroborates my presence log for the tax year.

[ ] **7. PR Address on All Official Documents**
My Puerto Rico address appears on my federal tax return, state filings (if any), financial accounts, and official correspondence.

## PAGE 4 — Income Sourcing & Exclusions (Items 8-14)

**Section: Income Sourcing & Federal-PR Coordination**

[ ] **8. IRC 933 Exclusion Properly Claimed [!]**
PR-source income excluded from my federal return under IRC 933 is genuinely sourced to Puerto Rico under applicable sourcing rules — not simply income received while living in PR.

[ ] **9. Federal-PR Income Reconciliation [!]**
My total worldwide income reconciles across my federal Form 1040 and PR Planilla (Form 482). Every dollar of income appears on one return or the other. No income is omitted from both.

[ ] **10. Capital Gains Properly Sourced [!]**
Capital gains reported at the preferential Act 60 rate are limited to post-move appreciation on eligible assets. Pre-move appreciation has been separately calculated and reported at applicable federal rates.

[ ] **11. Pre-Move Asset Valuations Documented**
I have fair market value documentation (appraisals, brokerage statements, or other records) for significant assets as of my PR residency start date, establishing the basis split between pre-move and post-move appreciation.

[ ] **12. No Double-Claimed Deductions**
Deductions and credits are claimed on either my federal or PR return — not both. I have reviewed for duplicate mortgage interest, charitable contributions, or business expense claims.

[ ] **13. Passive Income Correctly Categorized**
Rental income, dividends, interest, and other passive income items are sourced to the correct jurisdiction based on applicable rules (e.g., real property situs for rental income, not residence of recipient).

[ ] **14. Service Income Sourced by Performance Location**
Income from services is sourced based on where the services were performed, with appropriate documentation of work location for any services performed partially outside PR.

## PAGE 5 — Transfer Pricing & Entity Structure (Items 15-18)

**Section: Intercompany Transactions & Entity Compliance**

[ ] **15. Transfer Pricing Study Current [!]**
If I conduct business through related entities (e.g., a mainland US entity and a PR entity), I have a current, independent transfer pricing study supporting the intercompany pricing under IRC Section 482 standards.

[ ] **16. Intercompany Agreements in Writing**
All intercompany service agreements, licensing arrangements, or cost-sharing agreements are documented in written contracts with arm's-length terms.

[ ] **17. Economic Substance in PR Entity**
My PR entity has genuine economic substance — employees, office space, operational infrastructure — commensurate with the income it reports. The entity is not a shell receiving income without corresponding activity.

[ ] **18. Entity Registrations Current**
All business entities are current with their PR Department of State registration, municipal licenses (patente municipal), and any required DDEC filings.

## PAGE 6 — Decree Compliance & Reporting (Items 19-22)

**Section: Decree Terms & Annual Obligations**

[ ] **19. DDEC Annual Report Filed**
I have filed (or will file) the Annual Report to the Department of Economic Development and Commerce (DDEC) by the July 15 deadline, including all required financial disclosures and compliance certifications.

[ ] **20. Charitable Donation Requirement Met [!]**
I have made the required minimum annual donation ($10,000) to eligible Puerto Rico nonprofit organizations and have receipts documenting each contribution.

[ ] **21. Employment Requirement Reviewed**
I have reviewed my decree's specific employment creation requirements and can document compliance (or have documented an applicable exception with DDEC).

[ ] **22. Property Purchase Requirement**
If my decree includes a requirement to purchase residential property in PR within a specified timeframe, I have met this requirement or the deadline has not yet passed. I have documentation of the purchase.

## PAGE 7 — Foreign Account & Asset Reporting (Items 23-25)

**Section: International Reporting Obligations**

[ ] **23. FBAR Filed (FinCEN 114) [!]**
If I had foreign financial accounts with aggregate balances exceeding $10,000 at any point during the year, I have filed (or will file) FinCEN Form 114 by the applicable deadline. This includes foreign bank accounts, brokerage accounts, crypto exchange accounts held outside the US, and any accounts where I have signature authority.

[ ] **24. FATCA Reporting (Form 8938)**
If my foreign financial assets exceed the applicable reporting threshold ($200,000 for single filers living abroad / $400,000 for married filing jointly), I have filed Form 8938 with my federal return.

[ ] **25. Foreign Trust Reporting (Forms 3520/3520-A)**
If I am a grantor, beneficiary, or have any relationship with a foreign trust, I have filed (or confirmed filing of) Form 3520 and/or Form 3520-A as required. Penalties for non-filing can reach $10,000 per form per year.
